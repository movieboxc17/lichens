About Lichens

Lichens are symbiotic organisms composed of fungi and algae or cyanobacteria. They are known for their resilience in harsh environments and contribute significantly to ecosystem functions like soil formation and nitrogen fixation.

Images of Lichens

Lichen DNA and Genetic Studies

Studies of lichen DNA have unveiled their complex evolutionary history. The fungal partner largely determines the lichen's morphology and its ability to thrive in extreme conditions. DNA sequencing aids in understanding the symbiotic relationships within lichens.

To extract DNA from lichens, researchers typically use a method involving grinding the lichen material followed by chemical extraction processes to isolate the DNA. This process often includes using a buffer solution to break down cell walls and enzymes to digest proteins.

Interesting Facts about Lichens

  • Lichens can live for hundreds of years and grow on various surfaces like rocks, trees, and soil.
  • They serve as bioindicators of air quality due to their sensitivity to pollutants.
  • Certain lichens produce unique compounds used in medicine and dyes.
