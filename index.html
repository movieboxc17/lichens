About Lichens

Lichens are symbiotic organisms composed of fungi and algae or cyanobacteria. They are known for their resilience in harsh environments and contribute significantly to ecosystem functions like soil formation and nitrogen fixation.

Lichen DNA Extraction

Extracting DNA from lichens is a crucial step in genetic studies of these complex symbiotic organisms. The process typically involves several key steps to isolate high-quality DNA for further analysis.

The general steps for lichen DNA extraction include:

  1. Sample preparation: Carefully cleaning and preparing the lichen sample to remove contaminants.
  2. Cell lysis: Breaking down the cell walls and membranes to release the DNA.
  3. DNA isolation: Separating the DNA from other cellular components.
  4. Purification: Removing any remaining impurities from the extracted DNA.
  5. Quality assessment: Checking the purity and concentration of the extracted DNA.

The specific method used can vary depending on the lichen species and the research requirements. Researchers may employ techniques such as CTAB (cetyltrimethylammonium bromide) extraction, silica-based methods, or commercial DNA extraction kits optimized for plant or fungal tissue.

Interesting Facts about Lichens

  • Lichens can live for hundreds of years and grow on various surfaces like rocks, trees, and soil.
  • They serve as bioindicators of air quality due to their sensitivity to pollutants.
  • Certain lichens produce unique compounds used in medicine and dyes.
